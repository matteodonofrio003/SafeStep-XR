# Interaction Sample Project

Open the **Assets** folder and navigate inside **LeonardoXR SDK/1.0.0/Core Samples**, here you will find different sample scenes and assets showcasing LeonardoXR's different features.

For example the **Interaction Scene** scene is a simple interaction scene, pre-configured for controller/hands usage, in which the player will assemble an engine.


![](imgs/sample_project/TP_open.png)

 
[#](.) Build and Run
--------------------

Now, let's build and run our first app on the LeonardoXR.

1. Make sure you have [enabled Developer Mode](Utilities%20and%20Tips.md#enabling-developer-mode) on the LeonardoXR.

2. Connect the LeonardoXR to your computer with a USB cable.

3. Select **File - Build Settings...** from the Unity main menu.

4. Make sure that the **Interaction Scene** and the **Hand Gestures Scene** are listed in the **Scene List** box.

5. In the **Build Settings** window, select your LeonardoXR in the **Run Device** field.

6. If your device is not showing up in the list, click on the **Refresh** button.

7. Click on the **Build and Run** button.

	![](imgs/sample_project/TP_build.png)

8. In the **Build Android** window enter **SDKDemo.apk** into the **File name** field.

9. Unity will start building the application.

10. Your **SDKDemo** app should launch on your LeonardoXR. If the app does not automatically launch, you can launch it manually in the Apps tray.
